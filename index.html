Overall, major crime in the Bronx continues to decrease

A look at the percent change in major crime in the Bronx and how it compares to New York City crime overall from 2000-2022. Which crimes continue to decrease and which have increased?

By Eileen Street
Nov. 30, 2023

Bronx, N.Y.––The Bronx has struggled with its reputation through the decades, in part with high crime rates. However, historical New York City crime data, made up of reported crime and offense data recorded by the New York City Police Department (NYPD), paints a different picture.

The seven major felony offenses, according to NYPD, are murder, rape, robbery, felony assault, burglary, grand larceny, and then grand larceny with an automobile. Looking at the percentage change of these seven major felony crimes from 2000-2022, five of seven major crimes still have a lower number of offenses compared to 2000. The graphs below also include the percentage change of the major crimes from 2000-2022 for New York City (including Bronx to show all five boroughs).

The five major crimes that remain below 2000 level offenses

“What is always most interesting about these types of charts is how NYC is consistent between boroughs,” said Christopher Herrmann, an associate professor at John Jay College of Criminal Justice at City University of New York. Herrmann, a former crime analyst supervisor with NYPD, added, “The trends in the Bronx (and other boroughs probably) mirror/reflect the overall Citywide trend.”

One major crime recently surpassed in number of offenses compared to 2000.

Grand larceny is the only major crime in the Bronx that hasn't dipped below 2000 levels in two decades.

Overall major crime is down in the Bronx and New York City as a whole, but offenses started increasing around the pandemic.

When looking at the charts, it’s important to keep in mind the data is based on crimes reported to police.

“We know that the public does not do a great job reporting crime, even serious/felony crimes. The exception to the crime reporting issues are murder and auto theft, otherwise, we know that the majority of serious crime (according to the National Crime Victimization Survey) does not get reported to local police departments and is not reflected in official statistics,” explained Herrmann.
